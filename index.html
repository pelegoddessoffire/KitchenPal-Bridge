Pork Tostadas with Salsa Verde

Servings: 2 (double for 4)

Notes: If you're cooking for 4 servings, double measurements accordingly.

Ingredients

  • 12 oz Green Cabbage
  • 7 oz Tomatillo
  • 1 Jalapeño
  • ¼ oz Cilantro
  • 4 oz Red Onion, Diced
  • 1 tbsp Garlic
  • 1 tbsp Orange Juice Concentrate
  • 4 Corn Tortillas
  • 1 tbsp Raw Sugar Pack
  • 4 tsp Spice Blend (⅛ tsp Ancho Powder, ½ tsp Chili Powder, 2 tsp Oregano, ½ tsp Cumin, ⅛ tsp Cinnamon; spicy, use with discretion)
  • 1 Avocado
  • ½ cup Feta
  • 10 oz Sliced Pork
  • Salt & Pepper to taste
  • Oil as needed
  • ¼ cup Water

Instructions

  1. Turn broiler to 500°F with rack 6 inches from the top. Line a baking sheet with foil. Wash tomatillo, cabbage, jalapeño, and cilantro. Dice tomatillo into ¼ inch pieces and place in a bowl. Chop cilantro and add. Halve jalapeño, remove stem and seeds, chop finely. Pat pork dry.
  2. Add half onions and garlic to the bowl with tomatillos. Add jalapeño and salt, stir. Set aside.
  3. Brush tortillas with oil on both sides. Broil 2-4 minutes, flipping halfway, until crisp. Monitor to prevent burning.
  4. Heat skillet over medium. Cook pork, remaining onions, and garlic 2-3 minutes, pulling apart. Season, cook until no longer pink. Add juice, sugar, spice, water; cook until 165°F.
  5. Slice avocado, mash on tostadas. Add cabbage, pork, salsa verde, feta, and sauce drizzle. Enjoy!

Equipment

  • Baking Sheet
  • Foil
  • Medium Bowl
  • Large Non-Stick Skillet
  • Tongs
  • Oven/Broiler
